IKEA GRILLPLATS Energy Monitoring in Home Assistant

GRILLPLATS can report live power and accumulated energy to Home Assistant. Add or share the plug from Home Assistant's Matter screen; if you only see an on/off switch, the connection path is the first thing to fix.

TLDR

  • Use Home Assistant's Matter screen: add GRILLPLATS there, or share it from the smart-home platform that already owns it, for the best chance of exposing power and energy data.
  • DIRIGERA is optional for Home Assistant: you still need a Thread border router—a compatible hub that connects the plug's wireless network to your home network. DIRIGERA, Home Assistant Thread hardware, a HomePod mini, or a Nest Hub (2nd gen) can fill that role.
  • Avoid the current Zigbee route for metering: Zigbee is a separate low-power device network, and current Home Assistant testing shows basic switching but missing energy measurements.
  • Main caveat: sensor names, update timing, and platform displays can vary. Check the exact regional load rating before connecting an appliance; the US model has a separate 300 W motor limit.

The Short Answer: Add the Plug Through Home Assistant Matter

Yes, IKEA GRILLPLATS can show both power and energy in Home Assistant. Pair it directly with the Home Assistant phone app, or share the already-paired plug into Home Assistant as a Matter device. This uses the official Matter integration—the built-in Home Assistant connection for Matter products. On the device page, look for live power in watts (W) and accumulated energy in kilowatt-hours (kWh). Power is what the appliance is drawing now; energy is the total used over time.

If Home Assistant shows only an on/off switch, first check how the plug arrived there. A current r/homeassistant report found the energy sensors—called entities in Home Assistant—missing through a community DIRIGERA integration; the same plug exposed its monitoring after it was added through the Home Assistant Companion app. That report is not a guarantee for every firmware version, meaning every version of the plug's built-in software. It does match the official paths: IKEA advertises power tracking, Matter 1.3 defines energy reporting, and Home Assistant officially supports Matter devices.

IKEA's documented consumer setup for GRILLPLATS uses Matter over Thread. Matter is the common control language. Thread is the low-power wireless mesh carrying those commands. A Thread border router connects that mesh to the rest of your home network; it passes traffic but does not have to control the plug. You need that border router, but you do not specifically need IKEA's DIRIGERA hub.

Choose the Connection Path That Preserves Energy Data

The same plug can reach Home Assistant through several routes, but “the switch works” does not mean every route exposes the metering clusters—the standardized groups of device features. A controller is the smart-home platform that owns and operates the plug. A bridge is a hub that republishes devices behind it. Multi-admin is Matter's way to share one device with more than one controller. “Local” means normal commands stay inside the home instead of making a round trip through a vendor's cloud.

Best Setup: Add GRILLPLATS Directly to Home Assistant

1. Prepare the Supported Matter Path

Use current Home Assistant OS, the official Matter Server app, and the latest Home Assistant Companion app on your iPhone or Android phone. Home Assistant documents the Matter Server app on Home Assistant OS as its supported installation. A self-managed Matter Server container is an advanced, unsupported path, so reproduce a problem on the supported route before blaming the plug.

Confirm that a Thread border router is already working on the same reachable home network. Concrete choices include a Home Assistant Yellow or Connect ZBT-2 configured to provide Thread, IKEA DIRIGERA, Apple HomePod mini, Google Nest Hub (2nd gen), or Nest WiFi Pro. Owning one of these products is not enough by itself; its Thread role must be enabled and its local IPv6 path to Home Assistant must work.

2. Open Pairing and Scan the Unique Matter Code

When first powered, GRILLPLATS opens a 15-minute pairing window. The IKEA manual says the plug's on/off button can reopen pairing; use the documented factory reset only if a normal pairing window is not enough. Keep the included manual or a private backup photo because its Matter setup code is unique to that plug.

With the phone close to the plug, open the Home Assistant app and go to Settings → Matter → Add device. Choose No, it's new, scan the QR code, and follow the prompts. Bluetooth is used for this nearby setup handoff; normal operation then moves to Thread.

3. Confirm the Energy Sensors Before Building Automatic Routines

Open Settings → Devices & services → Matter, select GRILLPLATS, and inspect its entities—the controls and sensors Home Assistant creates for a device. Exact labels can change, but the useful groups are:

  • Power in W: the appliance's draw at this moment.
  • Energy in kWh: consumption accumulated over time.
  • Voltage and current: useful diagnostics, not a substitute for a certified electrical test instrument.
  • Switch: the relay that physically turns the outlet on or off.

Test with a modest, known load such as a lamp. A switched-off lamp or a low-power charger may correctly report zero or too little to make the cumulative kWh value move quickly. Give the total-energy entity time to change before deciding it is broken.

4. Add the Total to Home Assistant's Energy Dashboard

If the accumulated-energy entity is available as an individual-device source, add it under Settings → Dashboards → Energy → Individual devices. Use the kWh total, not the live watt reading, for long-term consumption. Home Assistant records the history and can show the plug beside other appliance totals.

Use GRILLPLATS to find patterns, not to settle a utility bill. IKEA promotes power tracking, but the product page does not make it a revenue-grade meter. Treat the data as household guidance unless the documentation for your exact regional model states a certified accuracy class.

If GRILLPLATS Is Already in IKEA, Apple, or Google Home

Do not factory-reset first. Matter can put one device on several controller fabrics—the separate secure networks owned by Home Assistant, Apple Home, Google Home, and other platforms.

  1. Update the Home Assistant Companion app and make sure the Matter integration is installed.
  2. In the controller that already owns GRILLPLATS, open its Matter sharing or “connect another service” flow.
  3. In Home Assistant, open Settings → Matter → Add device and choose Yes, it's already in use.
  4. Follow the handoff, then inspect the new Home Assistant Matter device for W and kWh entities.

If the existing IKEA or bridge route offers no sharing option and still exposes only switching, decide whether losing and rebuilding that setup is acceptable before resetting. Direct Home Assistant Matter pairing is the clearest route when Home Assistant energy monitoring is the priority.

What to Check When You Still See Only an On/Off Switch

  1. Check the integration name. The device should appear under Home Assistant's Matter integration, not only under a custom DIRIGERA or partially supported Zigbee path.
  2. Update the full path. Install stable updates for Home Assistant, Matter Server, the Companion app, and the plug when a trustworthy controller offers its firmware.
  3. Look at all entities. On the device page, open the entity list and check for disabled or unavailable diagnostic sensors before re-pairing.
  4. Use a real test load. A lamp gives an immediate watt reading; cumulative energy needs time.
  5. Confirm Thread and local IPv6. If the plug is unavailable rather than merely missing sensors, solve the border-router and home-network path first.
  6. Download Matter diagnostics. Save this support report of the plug's exposed features and software versions before any reset.

If direct Matter pairing itself fails, move the phone and plug near the active border router, keep Bluetooth on, and retry on a simple home network without guest-Wi-Fi isolation. As a last step, unplug the connected load and follow IKEA's documented reset: hold the on/off button for about 10 seconds until the red light stops blinking. The reset clears saved connections and data, so keep the QR code ready.

Do You Need DIRIGERA?

No—DIRIGERA is optional for direct Home Assistant control, but a Thread border router is required. DIRIGERA is required if you want to manage GRILLPLATS in the IKEA Home smart app. It can also bridge compatible IKEA devices to Home Assistant through Matter, although the features exposed by a bridge can be narrower.

Skip DIRIGERA when Home Assistant is already your main controller and you already have a reliable Thread border router. Home Assistant can commission GRILLPLATS directly, keep normal commands local, and share the plug onward to Apple Home or Google Home. Buying DIRIGERA does not automatically fix a weak Thread mesh or a blocked local IPv6 network.

Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, and SmartThings

IKEA lists GRILLPLATS as compatible with Amazon, Apple, Google, Homey, and Samsung systems. That statement covers ecosystem compatibility, not identical app screens. One platform may show a switch while another also presents watts, current, voltage, or accumulated energy.

For Apple users, this is a Matter accessory in Apple Home, not a classic HomeKit accessory. Home Assistant's HomeKit Device integration is therefore not the normal way to add it. Pair to Apple Home and share it to Home Assistant, or pair to Home Assistant first and use Share device to add Apple Home. The same multi-admin idea applies to other compatible Matter controllers, though account, voice, remote-access, and history features can still depend on each platform's cloud.

What Works Locally, and What May Still Use the Cloud?

Home Assistant's official Matter integration sends normal control and energy-reporting traffic inside the home. GRILLPLATS does not need IKEA cloud for Home Assistant to turn it on, read its Matter data, or run a local automation—an automatic routine. If the internet connection fails, those normal commands can keep working as long as the home network, Home Assistant, border router, and plug still have power.

Cloud services can still appear around that local core. The IKEA Home smart app is part of the DIRIGERA path. Apple, Google, Amazon, Samsung, or Homey accounts may provide remote access, household sharing, voice assistants, history, or firmware delivery. An internet outage can remove those extras even while local Home Assistant control continues.

Community Caveats Worth Knowing

  • Zigbee certification does not mean complete Home Assistant support: the CSA lists GRILLPLATS variants as Zigbee 3.0 certified, but IKEA's consumer page and manual document Matter-over-Thread setup. Current Zigbee2MQTT and ZHA discussions describe basic switching while metering is missing or unsupported.
  • Update timing varies: some owners report frequent readings; others report updates mainly around switching or odd zero-load voltage and frequency values. Treat these as firmware and controller observations, not guaranteed specifications.
  • Bridges can trim features: Home Assistant warns that a Matter bridge may not expose every capability of the device behind it. Confirm the entities you need before moving automations.
  • Custom Matter servers add variables: Home Assistant supports the official Matter Server app on Home Assistant OS. Containers and unusual Bluetooth or IPv6 stacks require operator troubleshooting.

Check the Regional Rating Before You Plug In an Appliance

Do not copy a rating from another country's GRILLPLATS. The US article 706.247.40 manual lists 120 V, 15 A and 1800 W for what it calls a resistive load—a category that does not cover motors—but a much lower 300 W maximum motor load. A refrigerator, pump, fan, or other motor is not covered by the 1800 W number. Check the label and manual supplied with your exact plug and the appliance's startup requirements.

  • Use the plug indoors and dry; do not use a damaged unit.
  • Do not cover it, daisy-chain it with another smart plug or power strip, or exceed the printed load rating.
  • Do not use a smart relay as the only disconnect for medical, life-safety, heating, refrigeration, server, pump, or other critical equipment.
  • Disabling the switch entity in Home Assistant reduces accidental dashboard taps; it is not a hardware interlock and does not remove the physical button or relay.

What to Avoid

  • Do not choose the current Zigbee route when power and energy reporting are the goal.
  • Do not assume DIRIGERA is mandatory when a working compatible Thread border router already exists.
  • Do not assume every platform exposes the same data just because the Matter logo is present.
  • Do not factory-reset before checking the connection path and trying Matter multi-admin sharing.
  • Do not treat a Home Assistant software switch as an electrical safety control.

Tara's Practical Recommendation

If Home Assistant is where you want energy history and automations, make its official Matter integration a direct controller for GRILLPLATS. Use one intentional Thread network, keep a powered border router near enough for a stable mesh, and confirm watts and kWh before creating appliance alerts.

Keep DIRIGERA only when its IKEA app, device-management, or remote-control role is useful to your household. Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, or SmartThings can remain additional controllers through Matter sharing. Put each automation in one chosen platform so the same plug is not being switched by duplicate schedules.

FAQ

Does IKEA GRILLPLATS show energy use in Home Assistant?

Yes. GRILLPLATS supports Matter energy reporting, and Home Assistant can expose live power and accumulated energy when the plug is commissioned or shared through the official Matter integration. Exact entity names and update timing can vary with software and firmware.

Does GRILLPLATS need IKEA DIRIGERA for Home Assistant?

No. DIRIGERA is one compatible Thread border router and IKEA hub, but it is optional for direct Home Assistant Matter setup. GRILLPLATS does require a working Thread border router, such as a correctly configured Home Assistant OpenThread border router, HomePod mini, or Nest Hub (2nd gen).

Can GRILLPLATS be added to Apple Home and Home Assistant?

Yes. Add it to either Matter controller first, then use Matter multi-admin sharing to add the other. GRILLPLATS uses Matter in Apple Home rather than the older HomeKit accessory protocol, and each platform may present a different subset of its energy data.

Does GRILLPLATS need the cloud for Home Assistant control?

No vendor cloud is required for normal Matter control and energy reporting inside Home Assistant. An IKEA, Apple, Google, Amazon, or Samsung account may still be used for that platform's app, remote access, voice features, setup, or firmware path.

Should I pair GRILLPLATS to Home Assistant with Zigbee?

Not if energy monitoring is the goal. The CSA lists GRILLPLATS variants as Zigbee 3.0 certified, but IKEA's consumer page and manual document Matter-over-Thread setup, and current ZHA and Zigbee2MQTT reports show basic on/off control with missing power measurements. Matter remains the safer recommendation for energy data.
