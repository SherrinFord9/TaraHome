Do You Still Need IKEA DIRIGERA With Home Assistant?

For the new IKEA Matter-over-Thread lineup, DIRIGERA is optional. It is still useful for older IKEA Zigbee gear, the IKEA Home smart app, IKEA-guided updates, and a simpler family-facing control surface.

TLDR

  • Direct answer: You do not need IKEA DIRIGERA for new IKEA Matter-over-Thread devices if Home Assistant has Matter, a Thread border router, and the right phone credentials.
  • What that means: Matter is the shared smart-home language; Thread is the low-power wireless network; the border router is the radio bridge into that network.
  • Keep DIRIGERA when: You have older IKEA Zigbee/TRADFRI devices, want the IKEA Home smart app, need IKEA's simplest support path, or want a backup way to control IKEA gear.
  • Avoid this: Do not assume adding DIRIGERA as a Matter bridge makes every new IKEA Thread device automatically appear in Home Assistant.
  • Main caveat: Matter can share devices across platforms, but duplicate automations and separate Thread networks make a simple home harder to support.

Short Answer: DIRIGERA Is Helpful, Not Mandatory

If your goal is to use new IKEA Matter-over-Thread devices in Home Assistant, you can skip DIRIGERA. Matter is the shared smart-home control language, Thread is the low-power wireless network used by many battery sensors, and a Thread border router is the radio bridge that connects that network to your normal home network. Home Assistant can be the Matter controller, and a Home Assistant Thread border router such as Connect ZBT-2, Connect ZBT-1, Home Assistant Yellow, or another supported OpenThread border router can carry the Thread network. In plain English: Home Assistant can talk to the device locally, and the IKEA hub is not the required middleman.

You should still keep or buy DIRIGERA when the IKEA ecosystem is part of the value. That means older IKEA Zigbee devices, IKEA scenes and app control, a simple non-Home-Assistant control surface for family members, or an IKEA-first update and troubleshooting path. Zigbee is the older low-power smart-home radio used by many pre-Matter IKEA devices. DIRIGERA also acts as a Matter bridge for older IKEA smart products, which is different from directly pairing a new Matter-over-Thread sensor to Home Assistant.

The practical recommendation is simple: let Home Assistant run the automations, and use DIRIGERA only when it solves a real IKEA problem. Do not build the house so every light, sensor, and button has competing routines in IKEA Home, Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, SmartThings, and Home Assistant at the same time.

Tara's rule: one automation brain, as few cloud assumptions as possible, and one documented pairing path for each device. Matter gives you sharing; it does not remove the need for discipline.

Why This Question Is Hot Right Now

IKEA's November 6, 2025 announcement said the company was launching 21 Matter-compatible smart home products across lighting, sensors, remotes, and plugs. The official IKEA Matter guide now says you do not need DIRIGERA for Matter devices, while still recommending it because it connects new and old IKEA gear, works as a Matter controller, and makes setup simpler.

That official nuance is exactly why Home Assistant owners are confused. In 2026, Home Assistant Community threads are asking whether to buy DIRIGERA or Home Assistant Connect ZBT-2 for IKEA Matter devices, why devices connected through DIRIGERA do not always show up in Home Assistant, and how to handle IKEA Matter firmware and BILRESA reliability. Reddit users are asking the same household question in less formal words: do I really need another vendor hub when Home Assistant is already the hub?

The answer depends on which job you want DIRIGERA to do. DIRIGERA can be a Matter controller for the IKEA Home smart app, a Thread border router, and a Matter bridge for older IKEA devices. Home Assistant can be the automation system, Matter controller, and Thread owner. Those roles overlap, but they are not the same thing.

What DIRIGERA Actually Does

DIRIGERA is not just an old-style gateway. IKEA introduced Matter bridge support for DIRIGERA on September 11, 2024, so existing IKEA Zigbee-based products connected to DIRIGERA could work with Matter-supported platforms. IKEA's newer smart-home guide also describes DIRIGERA as a Matter controller and says it includes Thread border router functionality for the new Thread-based sensors.

Those words matter:

  • Matter controller: the system that commissions and controls Matter devices. IKEA Home, Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, SmartThings, and Home Assistant can all play this role in different ways.
  • Thread border router: the radio path between low-power Thread devices and the normal home network. It forwards encrypted packets; it is not automatically the automation brain.
  • Matter bridge: a bridge exposes some non-Matter devices, such as older IKEA Zigbee devices, to a Matter controller.

Home Assistant's Matter documentation names IKEA DIRIGERA as an example of a Matter bridge. It also warns that a Matter bridge does not guarantee every device or every feature from that ecosystem will appear. That is the trap: bridging older IKEA Zigbee devices through DIRIGERA is not the same as automatically importing every new IKEA Matter-over-Thread device that happens to be visible in the IKEA app.

When to Skip DIRIGERA

Skip DIRIGERA when you are buying new IKEA Matter-over-Thread gear specifically for Home Assistant and you are comfortable making Home Assistant own the smart-home plumbing. This is the clean local-first path:

  1. Home Assistant OS, or a carefully supported Matter Server setup.
  2. The Matter integration in Home Assistant.
  3. A Thread border router that Home Assistant can use, such as Connect ZBT-2, Connect ZBT-1, Home Assistant Yellow, or another supported OpenThread border router.
  4. The Home Assistant companion app on the phone that will commission the device.
  5. A simple network where IPv6 and mDNS are not blocked by VLAN rules, multicast filters, or "smart" Wi-Fi settings.

With that in place, a MYGGBETT contact sensor, KLIPPBOK leak sensor, BILRESA remote, TIMMERFLOTTE sensor, ALPSTUGA air monitor, or KAJPLATS bulb can be treated like a Home Assistant device first. Home Assistant becomes the place where names, rooms, dashboards, and automations live.

This is also the path Tara prefers for installed local systems. It keeps the handoff understandable: the hub owns the automation, the Thread radio is documented, and a future support call does not need to reverse-engineer whether the device was first paired in IKEA Home, Apple Home, Google Home, or Home Assistant.

When to Keep DIRIGERA

Keep DIRIGERA when it solves a real household problem that Home Assistant alone does not solve as cleanly.

You have older IKEA Zigbee devices

Existing IKEA Zigbee/TRADFRI gear is the strongest reason. DIRIGERA's Matter bridge can expose older IKEA products to Matter platforms while preserving the IKEA app as the place where those products are maintained. If your home already has IKEA blinds, remotes, light drivers, plugs, or older bulbs working reliably on DIRIGERA, do not rip that out just because a new Matter sensor can pair directly to Home Assistant.

You want the IKEA Home smart app

Some homes need a simple, branded app for ordinary control. IKEA says the Home smart app handles rooms, scenes, routines, and control for the IKEA smart home. That can be useful for guests, family members, or a homeowner who does not want to open Home Assistant every time a remote, lamp, or blind needs attention.

You want the most IKEA-friendly support path

For brand-new IKEA devices, the official IKEA app and hub are still the easiest way to stay inside IKEA's documented support story. Home Assistant's Matter docs are clear that Matter and Thread are still areas where installation type, Thread credentials, IPv6, mDNS, and controller maturity can matter. If the person maintaining the home wants the fewest advanced troubleshooting terms, DIRIGERA can be a calmer choice.

You want a fallback control surface

Reddit demand around this topic often comes down to redundancy. A separate IKEA hub can keep some IKEA controls available if Home Assistant is down or a Home Assistant radio is being replaced. That does not mean every device should have duplicate automations. It means some homeowners value a second, simpler control path for lights, plugs, and remotes.

The Mistakes That Make This Setup Feel Broken

1. Treating DIRIGERA bridge mode as a universal importer

Adding DIRIGERA to Home Assistant as a Matter bridge can expose bridged devices, especially older IKEA Zigbee devices. It does not mean every new Matter-over-Thread device paired to IKEA Home will automatically become a full Home Assistant device. Several Home Assistant Community and GitHub reports exist because users expected Thread devices behind DIRIGERA to appear through the bridge path.

2. Creating the same automation in every app

A motion sensor can trigger a light in IKEA Home, Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, SmartThings, and Home Assistant. That does not mean it should. Duplicate automations cause double firing, delayed shutoff, mysterious overrides, and family confusion. Pick one automation brain. For a Tara-style local home, that is Home Assistant.

3. Accidentally creating separate Thread networks

Home Assistant's Thread docs say different vendors can create separate Thread networks with different credentials. A Thread border router is not automatically part of every other Thread network in the house. If Home Assistant, IKEA, Apple, Google, and Alexa each create separate networks, a small home can become harder to commission and troubleshoot than the old Zigbee setup it replaced.

4. Assuming firmware updates behave the same everywhere

Matter includes update support, but real update paths are still controller and vendor dependent. Home Assistant's Matter docs call out Thread OTA update limits in some Apple border-router situations. IKEA owners also discuss firmware and reliability issues in community threads. If updates are a major concern, decide which app or controller will be the update path before buying twenty devices.

5. Hiding the problem inside a clever network

Matter and Thread are local, but they are not magic. Home Assistant's Matter docs warn that IPv6 and mDNS multicast need to travel freely. In homeowner terms, mDNS is the local discovery traffic devices use to find each other, and VLANs are separate network zones that can accidentally block that traffic. If you are new to Matter-over-Thread, start on a simple flat home network before segmenting devices.

The Best Practical Setup for Most Home Assistant Homes

For a Home Assistant-first home buying new IKEA Matter devices in 2026, this is the cleanest plan:

  1. Use Home Assistant as the only place where serious automations live.
  2. Pair new IKEA Matter-over-Thread devices directly to Home Assistant when you have a Home Assistant-owned Thread border router.
  3. Share selected devices to Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, or SmartThings only when the family wants voice or familiar app control.
  4. Keep DIRIGERA for older IKEA Zigbee devices, IKEA app control, or IKEA-first support.
  5. Document which controller owns each device, which Thread network it joined, and where firmware updates are expected to happen.

This structure gives you the best of both worlds. Home Assistant stays local and powerful. Matter multi-admin still lets the family use other apps. DIRIGERA remains a tool for IKEA-specific value, not a mysterious mandatory box between every sensor and the automation system.

Tara's Interpretation

DIRIGERA is a good IKEA hub. It is not a requirement for every IKEA smart device in a Home Assistant house. The more important question is whether the homeowner wants an IKEA-centered smart home with Home Assistant attached, or a Home Assistant-centered smart home that happens to use affordable IKEA hardware.

For Tara installations, we prefer the second model. We use Home Assistant as the local brain, keep the radio plan simple, and avoid automations spread across five apps. DIRIGERA can still earn its place when there is older IKEA hardware, an IKEA support expectation, or a family app need. It just should not be bought out of fear that Matter-over-Thread cannot work without it.

FAQ

Do I need IKEA DIRIGERA for new IKEA Matter-over-Thread devices in Home Assistant?

No. You need a Matter controller, a Thread border router, and a phone that can commission the device with the right Thread credentials. Home Assistant can be that controller. DIRIGERA is optional when Home Assistant owns the Thread path.

Will IKEA devices paired to DIRIGERA automatically show up in Home Assistant?

Not always. Older IKEA Zigbee devices behind DIRIGERA can be exposed through bridge paths, but new Matter-over-Thread devices may need direct pairing to Home Assistant or Matter multi-admin sharing. Do not assume one DIRIGERA bridge pairing imports everything.

Should I buy DIRIGERA or Home Assistant Connect ZBT-2?

Buy DIRIGERA when you want the IKEA Home smart app, older IKEA Zigbee support, and IKEA's guided path. Buy or use a Home Assistant Thread radio when your goal is a Home Assistant-owned local system. Some homes can use both, but only if the pairing and automation ownership are documented.

Can I also use Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, or SmartThings?

Yes. Matter multi-admin lets a device be shared with more than one controller. Use that for family app control and voice assistants, not as an excuse to duplicate every automation.

Is this cloud-free?

Home Assistant control of Matter devices is local. DIRIGERA can control IKEA gear in the home, while IKEA Control Anywhere, voice assistants, mobile push behavior, and remote access can add cloud or account dependencies. Separate local control from remote convenience when deciding what matters during an internet outage.
